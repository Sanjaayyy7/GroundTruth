# Groundtruth — Evaluation Quality Model (v0.6)

This document defines what it means for an **evaluation itself** to deserve
trust, as ten separately measured dimensions. It is the human-readable
counterpart of the machine-derived quality manifest
([runs/quality-manifest.json](../runs/quality-manifest.json), regenerated by
`groundtruth audit` on every CI run).

**What this model refuses:** a composite quality score. Collapsing ten
dimensions into one scalar would exceed the evidence — the same refusal, for
the same reason, as per-scenario confidence intervals at n=8
(THREATS_TO_VALIDITY S1). The manifest reports each dimension separately with
a `status` of `machine` (derived from repository evidence), `documented`
(argued in a document, not yet machine-derived), or `gap` (not measured).

Each dimension below states: what it measures, why it matters, the objective
metric, how Groundtruth measures it today, and the remaining gap.

## D01 — Determinism
- **Measures:** whether identical inputs produce byte-identical outputs.
- **Why:** a finding that changes across runs cannot be audited or diffed.
- **Metric:** byte-identity of scorecards across repeated runs.
- **Today:** claim C9 (fact): demo agents guaranteed; llama3.2 byte-identical
  across both conditions from a fresh venv. Manifest field
  `D01_determinism` (register-tagged, `machine`).
- **Gap:** five of six models verified equal-scored, not byte-diffed (threat I5).

## D02 — Reproducibility
- **Measures:** whether a third party can regenerate published results.
- **Why:** unreproducible numbers are testimony, not evidence.
- **Metric:** documented reproduction tiers actually executed.
- **Today:** three-tier guide (REPRODUCIBILITY.md); fresh-venv reproduction
  executed at v0.5 (C9 external evidence marker records the session log,
  in-repo enforcement in tests/test_end_to_end.py). Field `D02_reproducibility`.
- **Gap:** reproduction log itself lives outside the repo; only its
  consequences are in-repo.

## D03 — Detector quality
- **Measures:** precision/recall of the instrument doing the judging.
- **Why:** an unvalidated judge converts noise into findings.
- **Metric:** micro P/R on the hand-labeled corpus + bootstrap 95% CI.
- **Today:** claim C6 carries machine-checked `metrics` (contract CT5 fails
  the build if the quoted numbers drift from runs/detector-quality.json);
  CI bounds from experiments/detector_quality_ci.py. Field
  `D03_detector_quality` (`machine`).
- **Gap:** corpus is single-annotator (see D04).

## D04 — Label quality
- **Measures:** trustworthiness of the ground-truth labels.
- **Why:** P/R is only as good as the labels it is measured against.
- **Metric:** annotator count; inter-rater κ.
- **Today:** threat S2 carries `attrs: {annotators: 1, kappa:
  blocked_on_second_annotator}` — surfaced honestly as the top scientific
  weakness. Field `D04_label_quality` (`machine` over the register).
- **Gap:** κ blocked on recruiting a second annotator.

## D05 — Dataset discriminative power
- **Measures:** whether scenarios actually separate subjects.
- **Why:** a scenario every subject passes (or fails) identically teaches nothing.
- **Metric:** per-scenario discrimination across the subject grid.
- **Today:** argued per scenario in DATASET_AUDIT.md (threat E5 tagged
  `dataset_discrimination`). Field `D05_dataset_discrimination`
  (`documented` — honest: not yet machine-derived).
- **Gap:** the discrimination matrix is prose; deriving it from scorecards is
  future work.

## D06 — Threat coverage
- **Measures:** how many known threats to validity are mitigated or resolved.
- **Why:** unexamined threats are silent assumptions.
- **Metric:** threat counts by status and category from threats.yaml.
- **Today:** 17 threats: 1 resolved (I2, evidence required by contract CT7),
  16 mitigated. Field `D06_threat_coverage` (`machine`).
- **Gap:** "mitigated" is self-declared; each names its future experiment.

## D07 — Statistical support
- **Measures:** which quantitative claims carry uncertainty bounds.
- **Why:** a point estimate without an interval overstates knowledge.
- **Metric:** claims with published CIs; statistics deliberately refused.
- **Today:** C6 carries the bootstrap CI (the one statistic 68 items
  support); per-scenario CIs refused as theater (S1). Field
  `D07_statistical_support`.
- **Gap:** macro-average alongside micro still pending (S3).

## D08 — Claim traceability
- **Measures:** fraction of claims whose evidence, falsification, and scope
  all resolve.
- **Why:** "why do you claim X?" must have an auditable answer.
- **Metric:** counts of claims passing contracts CT1/CT2 — counts, not a score.
- **Today:** 10/10 traced at the current commit. Field
  `D08_claim_traceability` (`machine`).
- **Gap:** traceability proves linkage, not truth — the linked experiments do
  that.

## D09 — Version stability
- **Measures:** agreement of harness commit, package version, and README.
- **Why:** numbers from mixed harness versions are not comparable (immutable #8).
- **Metric:** contract CT6 (commit exists in git; pyproject == README version).
- **Today:** enforced on every audit run. Field `D09_version_stability` (`machine`).
- **Gap:** README version extraction is a regex convention; a format change
  would surface as a loud CT6 failure, not silent drift.

## D10 — Experimental rigor
- **Measures:** whether conclusions were predicted before results existed.
- **Why:** pre-registration is the difference between testing a hypothesis
  and retrofitting one.
- **Metric:** git-ancestry verification that the predictions commit precedes
  the results commit.
- **Today:** C3's `preregistration` field is verified against git history by
  the loader (040c804 → 1bcfe31); the falsified P4 stays on the record.
  Field `D10_experimental_rigor` (`machine`).
- **Gap:** one pre-registered experiment so far; the practice is standing
  policy for future experiments.

## How to read the manifest

| Manifest key | This document |
|---|---|
| `dimensions.D01_…` … `D10_…` | one entry per dimension above |
| `contracts.definitions` | invariants CT1–CT10 (see docs/adr/0006) |
| `contracts.findings` | explanatory violations (empty when the build is green) |
| `registers.*` | claim/threat counts by classification/status |
| `graph.*` | evidence-graph surface (nodes/edges) |
| `unresolved_threats` | every threat not yet `resolved` |
| `no_composite_score` | the standing refusal, stated in the artifact itself |

The assurance report ([runs/assurance-report.md](../runs/assurance-report.md))
is the manifest's sibling: it answers *"what conclusions are justified?"* per
conclusion, never per repository.
